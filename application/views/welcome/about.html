The CARDIGAN project has drawn together computer scientists and life scientists from Oxford Brookes University, and clinicians from a Children's Hospital in Mexico to test whether Criticality Analysis can be applied to gait data to give a fast, reliable and cost-effective way to detect individuals at risk of developing type 2 diabetes.

Cardigan is coordinated by the School of Engineering, Computing and Mathematics and supported by the British Council Institutional Links Newton fund. Dr Tjeerd olde Scheper is the creator of the criticality analysis model and is analysing and processing the data with the support of researchers and Dr Clare Martin and Dr Arantza Aldea who also manages the project.



THE TEAM


Dr Arantza Aldea is a Senior Lecturer in the School of Engineering, Computing and Mathematics at Oxford Brookes University (UK). Previously she was a Lecturer at Universitat Rovira I Virgili in Spain and a post-doctoral researcher at Herriot Watt University (UK). She has worked in several international research projects and her research interests include knowledge representation, decision support systems and semantic web. She is currently interested in the use of smartphone technology and big data to facilitate the self-management of chronic health conditions.


Dr Tjeerd Olde Scheper is a Senior Lecturer at the School of Engineering, Computing, and Mathematics at Oxford Brookes University (UK). His research is in Dynamic Systems. He is particularly interested in the manner of which Biological Systems resolve problems in dynamics, and in learning to apply those concepts to Engineering, Medical and Biosystems problems. He developed and patented control method called Rate Control of Chaos, which limits the nonlinear dynamics of complex control problems into stable control domains. It has applications in the areas of lasers, combustion engines, HVAC, wind turbines, and bioreactors.



Dr Clare Martin is a Principal Lecturer at the School of Engineering, Computing, and Mathematics at Oxford Brookes University (UK). Her research interests include the use of mathematics to reason about the semantics and general algebraic properties of programming and specification languages. The areas of computer science that she is most concerned with are functional programming, relational programming algebras, and the theory and applications of predicate transformers and multirelations. She am also interested in the development and evaluation of intelligent mobile tools for the self-management of chronic health conditions, especially in relation to human computer interaction.


Prof Helen Dawes, Elizabeth Casson Trust Chair, leads the Movement Science Group based in the Faculty of Health and Life Sciences at Oxford Brookes University. Her research requires cross-disciplinary collaborations. Her activities include research, teaching and the provision of a Clinical Exercise and Rehabilitation in the community. Her research spans from exploring underlying mechanisms affecting performance through to service delivery of subsequently developed interventions and tools.



Dr Salvador Villalpando Carrion is the chief of gastroenterology at Hospital Infantil de Mexico Federico Gomez. He has More than 20 years of experience in the field of paediatric nutrition and gastroenterology in both clinical, educational and research areas. With experience in the dissemination and dissemination of science in mass media and social networks. In the academic area, which is the center of his activity, he is part of various groups of specialists and academics such as the Mexican Academy of Pediatrics, the Mexican Association of Pediatrics and the Faculty of Medicine of the UNAM.
